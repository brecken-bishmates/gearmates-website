Our Story

Gear that goes places
deserves better.

We built GearMates because the tools for managing your gear simply didn't exist — and the scattered mess of notes, spreadsheets, and half-remembered lists wasn't working for anyone.

The Why

"Your gear has a history. A story of every trip, every repair, every mile. It should have a home that remembers that."

We've all been there. The night before a trip, frantically trying to remember whether we repaired that sleeping bag zipper, whether the headlamp batteries are fresh, whether someone else is already bringing the tent. The mental overhead of gear management is real — and it grows every trip you take.

GearMates started as a simple idea: what if your gear inventory was as easy to maintain as a note on your phone, but as powerful as a proper tool that actually understands what you own, what shape it's in, and what you need for where you're going?

The AI Concierge took shape from a specific frustration — the gear audit you know you should do but never actually sit down to do. GearMates does it for you. Automatically. Before you even think to ask.

We built this for the people who care about their gear — not obsessively, but practically. The ones who own a good Osprey and keep it for years. The ones who remember the exact campsite where the rain jacket finally gave out. If that's you, GearMates was built for you.

What we stand for

Built on three things.

01

Honest

Your Gear Locker doesn't sugar-coat the state of your kit. If your Life Straw needs replacing before your next trip, GearMates will tell you — not wait until you're three days into the backcountry.

02

Practical

Not a social network. Not a shopping list. A real tool for real trips. We cut everything that doesn't make your preparation better, and we keep everything that does.

03

Warm

The app that feels like picking up a well-worn field journal — familiar, personal, lived in. Not sterile. Not corporate. The kind of tool that earns a permanent spot on your home screen.

Meet Gear

Your adventure companion.

Gear is a quokka — the notoriously happy-faced Australian marsupial. Always wearing a stuffed dark teal backpack with an orange water bottle in the side pocket. Ready for whatever comes next.

Gear shows up throughout the app as a companion, not a gimmick — celebrating your milestones, thinking through your planning, and occasionally sleeping in his backpack when there's nothing to do.

Good gear. Great company.
